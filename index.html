About Me

Science, music,
and the spaces between

I investigate questions at the intersection of neuroscience, AI, and human-centered care. At the Borner Lab, USC, I study the neural circuits controlling feeding behavior — work with implications for eating disorders and cancer-related anorexia.

Before that: exosome-based therapies for Alzheimer's, social stigma in psychiatric illness, music therapy for autistic children. A thread connects all of it — I'm drawn to how the brain shapes, and is shaped by, our emotional and social worlds.

I founded CogniBuddy, an AI home care platform for Alzheimer's patients, piloted in Nanjing in 2025. I also co-designed One Day, a board game helping Alzheimer's patients reconnect with family through play and memory.

I grew up between Shanghai and the world. I speak Mandarin, English, and Russian. I play piano on stage, paraglide, compose at midnight, and build tiny wool felt mice by hand.

One Day
Board Game

Longevity Design Challenge · 2024

🎲 Jun – Dec 2024

A family cognitive training game built around familiar home scenes — kitchen, bedroom, living room — where patients and families collaborate through memory, pattern recognition, and storytelling. Pipe-shaped pieces symbolize family connection; Memory Recovery Cards prompt sharing of real family photos.

Non-Drug Therapy Longevity Design Family-Centered 300+ Families Surveyed
🍳 Kitchen Scene
Easy: Count fruits on cards. Hard: "Choose cards for 4 cucumbers and 2 tomatoes." Trains visual recognition and logical categorization.
💡 Bedroom Scene
Easy: Identify shapes in lamp cards. Hard: Write a word prompted by the lamp — "Name something in the bedroom." Trains language memory.
🛋️ Living Room Scene
Easy: Name colors on carpet cards. Hard: Flip and match two cards — matching earns a puzzle piece. Trains short-term memory.
📷 Memory Recovery Cards
Families insert their own photos. Open-ended prompts help patients and families share stories and rebuild memories together.
🏆
Positive Emotional Feedback
Each completed task earns a puzzle piece. Patients feel achievement, boosting motivation to engage with family.
🏠
Real-Life Scenarios
Tasks mirror everyday moments — cooking, sorting, remembering — grounded in familiar home environments.
🎯
Improving Attention
Flipping, remembering, and matching cards helps patients improve focus through structured, joyful play.
